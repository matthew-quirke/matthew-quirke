## Matt Quirke

Marine / Electrical / Electronics / RF / Embedded systems

I work across real-world systems — fault finding, reverse engineering, and building reliable solutions in harsh environments (marine, RF, power systems). Most of my work sits at the intersection of electrical, electronics, and software rather than pure development.

### What I do
- Diagnose and repair complex electrical and electronic systems down to component level
- Investigate transient behaviour in DC power systems (LiFePO₄, inductive loads, switching systems)
- RF and radio troubleshooting (HF, VHF, AIS, SDR-based analysis)
- Embedded and Linux-based systems (STM32Fxxxx, Raspberry Pi, Arduino, custom builds, instrumentation)
- Design, install, and troubleshoot marine/off-grid power systems:
  LiFePO₄ battery banks, solar MPPT charging, hydrogenerators, and diesel generator integration
- Configure and diagnose Victron-based energy systems (charging behaviour, system faults, integration issues)

### Typical work
- Oscilloscope-based analysis of switching transients, MOSFET behaviour, and system faults
- RF signal investigation using SDR tools
- Marine electrical system design, upgrades, and fault finding
- Board-level electronics repair and diagnosis
- Python and Linux tooling for instrumentation, data capture, and automation

### Current focus
- Transient behaviour in marine DC systems
- MOSFET switching and gate driver behaviour
- Instrumentation and data extraction (SCPI, USBTMC, embedded interfaces)
- Building robust monitoring and control systems for real-world environments

### Background
Experience across marine engineering, electrical systems, electronics repair, and embedded/Linux environments. Comfortable working from high-level system design down to component-level troubleshooting.
